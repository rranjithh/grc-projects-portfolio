## 📊 Portfolio Projects

### 🔹 Project 1: Risk Assessment & Risk Register
**Fictional Company:** BrightPay Fintech (SaaS) | **Completed:** 2025-2026

**Objective:** Perform an end-to-end risk assessment for a fictional fintech SaaS company and produce a prioritised, actionable risk register.

**What I did:**
- Applied a 5×5 likelihood × impact scoring model across 10 identified risk scenarios (data breach, ransomware, insider threat, vendor failure, etc.)
- Built a colour-coded Excel risk register with heat map visualisation for executive-level reporting
- Selected and mapped controls to each risk: MFA, encryption at rest/transit, WAF, RBAC, and patch management
- Assigned risk owners and defined quarterly review triggers

**Outcome:** Residual risk scores dropped from an average of 18/25 to approximately 10/25 post-control implementation — representing ~60% reduction in high-risk exposure across the risk register.

**Key skills demonstrated:** Risk scoring methodology, Excel risk register, heat map, control mapping, residual risk calculation

### 🔹 Project 2: ISO 27001 ISMS Implementation
**Fictional Company:** CloudVault SaaS (Fictional) | **Completed:** 2025 - 2026

**Objective:** Design and document an ISMS aligned to ISO 27001:2022 for a fictional cloud SaaS provider, including a full gap analysis and Statement of Applicability.

**What I did:**
- Performed clause-by-clause gap analysis across ISO 27001:2022 Annex A control domains
- Identified 12 control gaps across access management, supplier relationships, and incident handling
- Prepared a Statement of Applicability (SoA) with justification for each control's inclusion or exclusion
- Built a remediation roadmap with timelines, owners, and priority tiers
- Drafted three core policies: Access Control Policy, Incident Response Policy, and HR Security Policy

**Outcome:** Gap count reduced from 12 to 4 following control implementation (~70% reduction in high-priority compliance gaps). Roadmap structured for a realistic 6-month certification readiness timeline.

**Key skills demonstrated:** ISO 27001:2022 Annex A, gap analysis, SoA, policy development, remediation planning

### 🔹 Project 3: Incident Response Plan
**Fictional Company:** NovaPay SaaS (Fictional) | **Completed:** 2025-2026

**Objective:** Develop a complete incident response lifecycle and validate it through a simulated ransomware tabletop exercise.

**What I did:**
- Designed a full IR lifecycle aligned to both ISO 27035 and NIST SP 800-61: Preparation → Detection → Containment → Eradication → Recovery → Lessons Learned
- Defined four severity tiers (P1–P4) with response SLAs and escalation thresholds
- Created RACI matrix assigning roles across Security, IT, Legal, and Executive teams
- Developed playbooks for three scenario types: ransomware, phishing, and data breach
- Conducted a tabletop simulation of a ransomware attack and documented findings

**Outcome:** Estimated MTTR improvement of ~50% versus an uncoordinated response, achieved by establishing pre-approved decisions, clear escalation paths, and documented containment procedures. Key gaps in communication and evidence preservation were identified and addressed.

**Key skills demonstrated:** IR lifecycle, playbook design, severity classification, RACI, tabletop exercise facilitation, MTTR analysis

### 🔹 Project 4: Vendor Risk Assessment
**Fictional Company:** TrustLine SaaS (Fictional) | **Completed:** 2025-2026

**Objective:** Build a vendor risk assessment framework and evaluate three vendors at different risk tiers.

**What I did:**
- Developed a 20-question vendor security questionnaire covering data handling, access controls, incident response capability, business continuity, and regulatory compliance (including DPDP Act 2023 requirements for India-based vendors)
- Created a 0–100 scoring model with weighted categories and automatic risk tier assignment (High/Medium/Low)
- Assessed three fictional vendors and produced individual risk scorecards
- Recommended contractual controls (right-to-audit clauses, breach notification SLAs, data processing agreements) for High-risk vendors
- Defined reassessment triggers: annual cycle + event-triggered review on breach or major contract change
- Integrated vendor risk findings into the enterprise risk register

**Outcome:** All three vendors classified and assigned follow-up actions. Framework designed to be repeatable and scalable across additional vendors without rework.

**Key skills demonstrated:** Vendor risk framework design, security questionnaire, scoring model, third-party risk management, DPDP Act 2023 awareness
